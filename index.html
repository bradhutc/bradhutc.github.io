I'm an incoming Physics & Astronomy Ph.D. student at the University of Pennsylvania and a Department of Energy Computational Science Graduate Fellow. I recently graduated from Indiana University with degrees in Astronomy/Astrophysics, Mathematics, and Physics.

My research sits where big survey astronomy meets representation learning. Modern telescopes image billions of objects through many filters — far too many for any human to inspect. I build wavelength-aware, unsupervised methods that let the data organize itself, surfacing rare and anomalous objects hiding in plain sight.

r-band · 623 nm · Research

Letting surveys sort themselves.

First-author work published in The Astronomical Journal and interviewed by the American Astronomical Society.

Publication · 2025

A Wavelength-aware Unsupervised Learning Approach for Large, Multicolor, Photometric Surveys

Hutchinson, B. D., Pilachowski, C. A., & Johnson, C. I.

The Astronomical Journal, 170, 255 · doi:10.3847/1538-3881/adf437

A compact, gated state autoencoder for multicolor photometry. Learned gates decide, object by object, which passbands carry signal and which are noise, compressing each source into a low-dimensional map where physical structure — and the anomalies that break it — emerge without labels.

Read the paper →

Anomaly detection

Finding the rare gems — objects that don't fit any known class — in billion-row photometric catalogs.

Gated representation learning

Tiny gated state autoencoders — a few hundred parameters — that reweight each passband per object and compress multiband light into interpretable, low-dimensional embeddings.
